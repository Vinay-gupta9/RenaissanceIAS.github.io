UPSC Prelims

Prelims is the first stage of examination and is important qualification stage to even attempt Mains and get in the race to secure a rank in the final list.

If you have a high enough hit rate or accuracy you can attempt 65 to 70 questions and clear the prelims by crossing the threshold.

About UPSC Prelims

However if your accuracy is low enough and you might have to solve more questions to balance out the negative marking.

So on an average the consensus is to solve around 80 to 85 questions for GS paper and not more.

Do's and Don'ts

Here are certain Do's and Don'ts as recommended by CSE toppers.

Do's

Fix your booklist and revise them multiple time.

Do not get lured by new materials in the market.

Create a revision schedule to ensure timely revision of your notes.

Solve test series as much possible. This helps in fillings the knowledge gaps.

Solve last few years question papers as well, they help you understand the way UPSC asks questions and give ample practice and revision for the static part.

Don'ts

Do not attempt all 100 questions.

Flukes for the sake of attempting more and more question can end up reducing your marks.

Do not leave any part of the syllabus.

The number of questions from a subject changes drastically.

Do not ignore CSAT because you have an degree in Engineering.

CSAT paper is a lengthy one and it difficulty level is increasing every year. So it requires practice.

Last 30 days to Prelims: How to deal with common issues ?

Prelims is just round the corner. There is no time to waste.

This is the time to Revise all that you have studied, practice some tests and chart out the strategy for the final exam if you have not done that yet.

Here, we discuss some common issues which are faced by the aspirants during the last lap to the prelims exam.

Issues Faced By Aspirants
  • Feeling of not being prepared enough
  • Low scores in tests
  • Some parts of syllabus still not covered
  • Have I covered enough current affairs?
  • Feeling not being prepared enough:
  • Even after you have prepared a lot for the prelims, you may sometimes feel that you have not done enough. It happens with most of aspirants even those who are already selected candidates. Many a times, it is related to comparison with your friends or marks/rank in a tests series. The only thing that you should take away from this, is that there is still a scope of improvement for you. Nothing more.
Points To Keep In Mind:
  • Focus on your progress and improvement in test papers by maximising marks in the subsequent papers
  • Quickly cover the areas/subjects that are weak without giving too much time. This way you will able to answer easy questions from this area.
  • Solve some Part test series of a particular subject in which you have good command to regain your confidence
  • Low scores in Prelims mock tests:
  • It is but obvious to get dejected with low scores in the mock tests. But, we have known people who have scored less marks in the Prelims mock test series of various institutes and still performed very well in the CSE Prelims Exam.
Action Points:

A.The key is not getting worried over low marks in a test and identify the areas in which you have not performed upto your mark to strengthen it.

Check if you are making any silly mistakes by not reading questions/options clearly.
